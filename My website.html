Education System in Japan

In Japan, higher education starts upon the completion of 12 years of education: elementary education (6 years of elementary school) and secondary education (3 years of lower secondary school and 3 years of upper secondary school).There are 5 types of higher education institutions that international students can be admitted to, which are 1) colleges of technology, 2) specialized training colleges(post-secondary courses), 3) junior colleges, 4) universities (undergraduate) and 5) graduate schools. Depending on the founding bodies, these higher education institutions are categorized into three types: national, local public and private.

School Uniform designs in Japan

School uniforms are a significant part of the Japanese education system and reflect the country's cultural, historical, and social values. There are various types of uniforms across schools, and these differences stem from historical roots, cultural ideals, and evolving fashion interests among younger generations.


Types of School Uniforms in Japan

  1. Sailor Fuku (Sailor Uniform)

    2. This style originated in the late 19th century, inspired by British naval uniforms, and was traditionally worn by female students. It consists of a blouse with a sailor-style collar and a pleated skirt. Some schools still use it today.

  2. Gakuran (Boysâ€™ Military-Style Uniform)

    3. Inspired by Prussian military uniforms, this is traditionally worn by male students. It includes a high-collared black jacket and matching trousers. Some schools continue to use it.

  3. Blazer Uniform

    4. From the 1990s onwards, more modern, stylish blazer-style uniforms became popular, especially in private schools. They are often seen as more fashionable and contemporary.

  4. Jumper Skirt Uniform

    5. A one-piece dress usually worn in warmer seasons. Less common today but still found in some schools.

  5. Genderless Uniforms

    6. Recently, some schools have adopted gender-neutral uniform policies to promote inclusivity and allow students more freedom in what they wear.
